About Me

My name is Cole, and I am a physicist turned Web Developer. During my time as a student at college, I worked as a research assistant for the McDermott lab at UW-Madison. It was in that lab I would write my first lines of code, and learn a variety of skills from designing circuits to soldering, and even machining. While I never ended up finding a job that really spoke to me within the physics field, it did introduce me to coding, where a lot of the skills in math and physics ended up helping. Writing the movement for the ball in the game above was actually the easiest part. Calculating velocity vectors, accounting for drag, accurate reflection angles, and momentum were all features I wasn't scared of implementing, and it felt good to still benefit from all those problems I solved in college.

Web Development won me over for a few reasons, it's possibly the most accesible platform for development. Billions can access any web page within minutes. I want people to be able to view my work, and being able to reach so many people with such ease is really appealing to me. On top of this, it scatches a creative itch I sometimes forget to scratch. Designing SVGs and playing with Figma in between writing actual code is a nice break from frustrating bugs and errors. The work flow feels balanced with both creative and analytical problems to solve, this also helps prevent burnout for me. Lastly, I love Javascript, I appreciate being able to work in the same language on front-end and back-end like I did in my Social Media Project you can view above, I also like some of the features of the langauge such as coercion.
